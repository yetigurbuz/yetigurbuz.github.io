Automatic Road Detection from Gray-level Images in Wide Area Surveillance
(ieee.org)
O. Can, Y. Z. Gurbuz, A. A. Alatan, IEEE International Symposium on Geoscience and Remote Sensing (IGARSS), 2016

In this work, we address the challenge of automatic road detection over large-scale, grayscale wide-area surveillance imagery by proposing two novel approaches. The first approach constructs a road probability map through tubularity estimation and parallel line detection, effectively leveraging the road’s inherent curvilinear structure and parallel edges to score each pixel’s likelihood of belonging to a road. Subsequently, the second approach refines these detections by building a graph over SLIC superpixels and exploiting graph-cut optimization to reconcile large intensity variations, shadows, and discontinuities. Although the tubularity–parallel-line pipeline is notably faster and yields promising recall rates, the graph-cut approach exhibits superior precision—even in mountainous areas—at the expense of higher computational load. Together, these techniques demonstrate robust performance in extracting extensive road networks from high-resolution grayscale imagery, thereby enabling wider surveillance coverage than conventional RGB-based methods while maintaining reliable accuracy and manageable runtime on datasets encompassing tens of thousands of pixels.
