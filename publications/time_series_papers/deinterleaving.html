Blind Deinterleaving of Signals in Time Series with Self-Attention Based Soft Min-Cost Flow Learning
(ieee.org)
O. Can, Y. Z. Gurbuz, B. Yıldırım, A. A. Alatan, IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing (ICASSP), 2021

We propose an end-to-end neural framework for blind deinterleaving of multiple radar signals in time series by casting the problem as a min-cost flow. Our method introduces a bi-level optimization that learns an association-cost function to separate interleaved pulse streams—even under missing pulses, strong jitter, and staggered modulation types. Instead of explicitly solving the min-cost flow, we build a self-attention-based architecture and incorporate soft flow constraints via novel penalty terms, leading to a trainable soft min-cost flow model. Extensive experiments on large-scale synthetic and real-world radar data validate the proposed approach, demonstrating superior accuracy compared to conventional histogram-based deinterleaving methods while robustly handling complex PRI patterns.
