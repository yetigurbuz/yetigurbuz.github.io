Deep Metric Learning with Chance Constraints
(arXiv)(code)
Y. Z. Gurbuz, O. Can, A. A. Alatan, preprint (arXiv Preprint), 2022

Bringing a different perspective to deep metric learning (DML) formulation, we formulate it as a chance constrained optimization problem and theoretically show that a contrastive loss based DML objective is a surrogate for the chance constraints. We rigorously convert the initial problem formulation into another form enabling expressing DML as a set intersection problem. The theory suggests a set intersection mechanism yet allows a greedy algorithm via iterative projections. To this end, we also relate the solution of a proxy-based DML approach to one of the supersets to be intersected to obtain the desired solution. As a result, we formally develop a proxy-based method that inherently employs arbitrary number of proxies for better generalization, realizing the mechanism suggested by the theory with a simple, yet effective, algorithm.
